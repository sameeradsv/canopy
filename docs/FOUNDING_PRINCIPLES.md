# Founding Principles

## Retrieval Quality > Storage Quantity

The value of the system comes from:
- meaningful recall,
- contextual retrieval,
- and pattern surfacing.

Not endless data collection.

---

## Sparse High-Signal Capture

Log:
- anomalies,
- repeated behaviors,
- recurring operational burdens,
- important decisions,
- or emotionally expensive events.

Avoid constant surveillance-style tracking.

---

## Human-In-The-Loop

Canopy may:
- summarize,
- surface patterns,
- provide suggestions,
- or reduce mental load.

The human remains the final decision-maker.

---

## Preserve Uncertainty

People are contextual and dynamic.

Avoid:
- rigid labels,
- permanent judgments,
- deterministic interpretations.

Support:
- confidence,
- reversibility,
- decay,
- and ambiguity.

---

## Local-First by Default

Sensitive data should remain under user control.

Prioritize:
- local storage,
- encryption,
- offline capability,
- optional sync,
- and minimal third-party dependency.

---

## Minimize Friction

Capture must remain lightweight.

If the system becomes operationally expensive, it will fail.

Target:
- under 30-second capture flows,
- progressive metadata enrichment,
- and automated contextual linking.
